Example Project 1

In this project, I employed mechanistic modeling and machine learning to predict protein-PEGylation outcomes. This approach is applicable to both graft-from (initiator) and graft-to (PEGylation) strategies. Additionally, I successfully predicted the specific lysine residue that would first react in an NHS-based reaction.

Skill highlights: machine learning, mechanistic modelling and simulation

Unveiling mechanisms to boost CHO cell growth and titer

Using a model molecule that is discovered to improve cell growth and titer, I used several assay techniques to enhance the role of mTOR and Akt pathways in improving CHO cell proliferation and ultimately titer by 1.5-2 fold.


Skill highlights: qPCR, Western blot, DNA damage assay, ROS assay, molecular docking

Re-engineering lysozyme solubility

Together with collaborators at Queen's University, I used molecular dynamics simulation to investigate mechanisms of lysozyme solubility alternation when complexed with surfactants. SDS specifically altered lysozyme structure, making its catalytic pocket more solvent-exposed.

Skill highlights: Linux (Windows Ubuntu environment), molecular dynamics simulation

Effect of cellular stress on bioprocess

I used various multivariate analysis methods along with machine learning to understand the effect of oxidative and ER stress on CHO cells. Findings include the negative effect of cysteine protein quality, the unexpected effect of IgG fragmentation on galactosylation, and correlations with both stresses and product quality.

Skill highlights: multivariate analysis (PCA, PLS), machine learning, design of experiments (DOE).

Investigating the effect of tau protein localization on aggregation propensity

Here I use molecular cloning to create tau proteins localized to different sub-cellular compartments (ER, cytosol, etc.). Using Western blotting and kinetic assays, I was able to understand the effect of different post-translational modifications (as a result of residing in different cellular locations) affect tau protein aggregation. The project contributes to a better understanding of tau protein fibrillization in Alzheimer's disease.

Skill Highlights: molecular cloning, Western blotting, Microscopy imaging, and analysis
